### — Material class split: farmable vs crafted

**Asked:** Two classes of throwable materials: farmable (trees, rocks) are weak when thrown. Built products (fences, walls, houses) do good damage based on weight.

**Done:**
- Added `"farmable"` as a third `item_category` value alongside `"material"` and `"crafted"`.
- Reclassified `obj-cherry-tree` and `obj-stone-block` from `"crafted"` to `"farmable"` in `objects.json`.
- Set `throw_effectiveness: 0.25` on both (down from 1.0). All other placeable objects remain `"crafted"` at 1.0.
- Updated descriptions for cherry-tree and stone-block to reflect their nature as raw/farmable objects.
- Updated content `CLAUDE.md` to document all three categories with the design intent table.

**Files changed:**
- `content/objects/objects.json` — cherry-tree and stone-block: category and effectiveness updated, descriptions rewritten.
- `content/CLAUDE.md` — `item_category` section expanded with farmable category, design intent documented.

**Decisions:**
- **Why `farmable` vs reusing `material`?** `material` is for raw drops (stone-chunk, wood-plank) — non-placeable, stackable resources. `farmable` is for naturally occurring placeable world objects (trees, raw stone blocks). Merging them would blur the distinction between "pickup items" and "world objects you can place and throw."
- **Why 0.25 effectiveness?** Consistent with raw materials. At current balance (STR 15, THROW_MULTIPLIER 1.4), a cherry tree deals ~28 damage and a stone block ~32 — compared to a flower pot (lightest crafted) at 33. The gap is small now but widens as throw_damage_base grows at higher tiers and with perk multipliers.
- Stone-wall was intentionally kept as `crafted` — it's a dressed, shaped, and assembled wall section, not raw stone.

**Notes:**
- Smash damage formula also reads throw_effectiveness, so smashing with farmable items is also penalised. This is correct and intentional.
- The cherry-tree description was updated to remove combat framing ("scatters petals and damage") — farmable items should feel like improvised options, not weapons.
